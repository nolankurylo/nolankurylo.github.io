A Little About Me


I was born in Calgary, AB and grew up playing many different sports. I played a little bit of football, golf and soccer, but hockey was my main sport.

Before entering high school, I chose to pursue hockey as my main sport. After completing high school, I moved on to play Junior Hockey in the Alberta Junior Hockey League (AJHL) for the Canmore Eagles, as well as the Kootenay International Junior Hockey League (KIJHL) for the Kimberley Dynamiters.

I learned a lot during my time playing hockey. I believe the sport has helped improve my respect, manners and interpersonal skills with others. I consider all of these traits to be valuable in any profession, whether it be hockey or Software Engineering.

Before the end of my hockey career, I had to make a decision in continuing to play hockey or start my education in pursuit of an engineering degree. Upon entering university, I knew that I wanted to do engineering but unsure of the specific field.

After taking an introductory course to the fundementals of C programming, I fell in love with the idea to program systems, specifically Embedded Systems. Since my decision to pursue the Software Engineering discipline, I have never looked back. I am eager to continue my education and obtain experience to further my development!
